## Q2: CCUS Technology

*"Which companies have installed, operated or tested carbon capture, utilisation or storage (CCUS) technology?"*

Both models give the same answer: **all four companies**.

---

### Mistral Large 123B (4-bit)

**JSW Steel** — Operates a CCU facility with 100 TPD capacity where CO₂ is captured and refined for use in the food and beverage industry [JSW Steel\_Sustainability\_2025.pdf, p.14]. Collaborates with technology and engineering companies to explore further CCUS options [p.14].

**JFE Holdings** — Participates in a national project to evaluate materials for CCUS. Studies practical application of CCUS technologies to reduce CO₂ from the blast furnace process [JFE Holdings\_Sustainability\_2025.pdf, p.120]. Collaborates with partners to develop carbon recycling supply chains and CCUS value chains [pp.72, 73, 123].

**POSCO** — Conducted a demonstration project capturing and utilising CO₂ in the steelmaking process, increasing the calorific value of Coke Oven Gas (COG) by 7% [POSCO\_Sustainability\_2024.pdf, p.13]. Plans to further utilise CCUS technology to reduce steelmaking CO₂ [p.14].

**Tata Steel UK** — Invested in R&D of carbon capture, use, and storage technologies [Tata Steel\_Sustainability\_2025.pdf, pp.10, 49, 51]. Participates in Project SUSTAIN to develop carbon capture and utilisation technologies for the steel industry [p.51].

---

### Llama 3.1 70B (4-bit)

**JSW Steel** — Operates a CCU facility with 100 TPD capacity, capturing CO₂ for use in the food and beverage industry [JSW Steel\_Sustainability\_2025.pdf, p.14].

**JFE Holdings** — Collaborated with partners to develop and test CCUS technologies, including a demonstration test for carbon recycling blast furnace technology [JFE Holdings\_Sustainability\_2025.pdf, pp.72, 123]. Established a joint evaluation agreement to build a CCS value chain [p.73].

**Tata Steel UK** — Mentions carbon capture, utilisation, and storage as part of its roadmap to CO₂-neutral steelmaking [Tata Steel\_Sustainability\_2025.pdf, p.50]. Invested in a project to develop a carbon recycling supply chain [p.51].

**POSCO** — Conducted a national R&D project with RIST (Pohang Institute of Industrial Science and Technology) since 2021, demonstrating CO₂ capture and utilisation technology [POSCO\_Sustainability\_2024.pdf, p.13]. Confirmed that low-purity CO₂ can be used in coke ovens without high-purity refining [p.13].

## Q3: Net-Zero Targets

*"What is the net-zero target of each company?"*

Both models are in agreement on Tata Steel UK and JFE Holdings. JSW Steel and POSCO 2050 targets were not retrieved in the available context excerpts.

---

### Mistral Large 123B (4-bit)

**Tata Steel UK** — Net-zero steelmaking by **2045** [Tata Steel\_Sustainability\_2025.pdf, p.50]. Interim target: 30% CO₂ reduction by 2030 [p.48].

**JFE Holdings** — Carbon neutrality by **2050** [JFE Holdings\_Sustainability\_2025.pdf, p.115]. Interim target: at least 30% reduction in GHG emissions by FY2030 vs FY2013 [p.60].

**JSW Steel** — Specific net-zero target year not retrieved in available excerpts. Interim target: 42% CO₂ emission reduction by 2030 vs 2005 baseline [JSW Steel\_Sustainability\_2025.pdf, p.14].

**POSCO** — Specific net-zero target year not retrieved in available excerpts [POSCO\_Sustainability\_2024.pdf].

---

### Llama 3.1 70B (4-bit)

**Tata Steel UK** — Carbon neutrality by **2045**, with 30% CO₂ reduction by 2030 and 90% reduction of direct CO₂ emissions [Tata Steel\_Sustainability\_2025.pdf, p.50].

**JFE Holdings** — Carbon neutrality by **2050**, with 30% GHG reduction by 2030 and development of ultra-innovative technologies [JFE Holdings\_Sustainability\_2025.pdf, p.115].

**JSW Steel** — Target year not explicitly retrieved. Committed to 42% CO₂ reduction by 2030 with a goal of achieving 1.95 tCO₂/tcs [JSW Steel\_Sustainability\_2025.pdf, p.4].

**POSCO** — Target year not explicitly retrieved in available excerpts [POSCO\_Sustainability\_2024.pdf].

## Known Limitations in These Answers

**Q1 — JFE Holdings:** The Scope 1+2 table on PDF p.259 contains multiple row groups (JFE Steel Group, JFE Steel standalone, subsidiaries). Both models retrieved this page but selected inconsistent rows. Verified ground truth values (from direct PDF inspection): Scope 1 FY2023 = 47.4 Mt, FY2024 = 44.0 Mt; Scope 2 FY2023 = 7.2 Mt, FY2024 = 7.0 Mt; Scope 3 FY2023 = 22,701 kt, FY2024 = 21,894 kt; Energy FY2023 = 612 PJ, FY2024 = 567 PJ [pp.259–263].

**Q1 — JSW Steel number notation:** JSW reports in Indian lakh notation (5,31,00,751 = 53,100,751). Both models preserved this notation without conversion. Verified ground truth: Scope 1 FY2023-24 = 52,106,566 t, FY2024-25 = 53,100,751.63 t; Scope 2 FY2023-24 = 1,061,079 t, FY2024-25 = 1,653,056.65 t; Energy FY2023-24 = 517,690,735 GJ, FY2024-25 = 529,209,356.78 GJ [pp.13–15].

**Q1 — POSCO year alignment:** Mistral Large returned 2024 Scope 1/2 values for 2023. Verified ground truth: Scope 1 2023 = 70,588,012 tCO₂e, 2024 = 69,665,353 tCO₂e; Scope 2 2023 = 1,383,895 tCO₂e, 2024 = 1,399,825 tCO₂e; Energy 2023 = 354,002,733 GJ, 2024 = 359,242,804 GJ [pp.139, 154].

**Q1 — Tata Steel UK year assignment:** Mistral Large returned year-shifted values; Llama 70B returned empty rows. Verified ground truth (all on PDF p.52): Scope 1 FY23 = 6.1 Mt, FY24 = 5.9 Mt, FY25 = 2.4 Mt; Scope 2 all years = 0.1 Mt; Scope 3 FY23 = 1.7 Mt, FY24 = 1.8 Mt, FY25 = 4.4 Mt; Energy FY23 = 71,848 TJ, FY24 = 71,234 TJ, FY25 = 35,174 TJ.

**Q3 — JSW Steel and POSCO 2050 targets:** Both companies have stated net-zero targets (JSW: "Net Neutral by 2050" [JSW Steel\_Sustainability\_2025.pdf, p.4]; POSCO: "Carbon Neutral by 2050" [POSCO\_Sustainability\_2024.pdf, pp.13–15]) that were not surfaced in retrieval for Q3. The interim 2030 target text appears on the same pages as the emissions tables, which dominate Q3 retrieval ranking. This is a known retrieval failure documented in APPROACH.md.
